Overview

In Spring 2026, TLC offered Nurturing Engaged and Ethical Learners with Critical AI Literacy as an in-person session in the Preparing to Teach series (May 4, 2026). The workshop focused on critical AI literacy, ethical assignment design, and integrating reflection on affective outcomes when students use AI. 5 participants completed the post-workshop survey (19% of 27 RSVPs).

The session introduced frameworks for guiding students to reflect critically on AI use, with an emphasis on affective outcomes alongside technical skills. Participants explored card-based activities and resources for designing assignments that nurture ethical, engaged learners.

Who participated

Respondents were UCLA instructors and instructional team members who attended the in-person workshop. Of 27 RSVPs, 5 completed the survey and 1 additional partial response(s) were recorded. Feedback reflects faculty perspectives on AI-integrated assignment design and ethical learning outcomes.

Survey responses were anonymous. No personally identifying information is included in this summary.

Suggestions

From participants
“Longer session”
“Very well organized! Thank you”
“The workshop felt as though it had two somewhat related goals that couldn't fully be addressed in the hour. As far as I understood it, the core insight of the workshop was that guiding students to reflect critically on AI should include a consideration of affective outcomes. This is a powerful point, but I think most participants came in expecting to tweak an assignment, and so it was hard in the workshop activities to really get a sense of the big picture. The use of multiple types of card decks may have exacerbated the confusion: I really liked learning that these resources existed, but I wonder if thinking about values versus thinking about specific assignments that engage with AI really amounts to two different workshops? But I do appreciate the TLC's programming on these issues!”
Themes for next time
  • Offer a longer session or split content into two focused workshops (values/reflection vs. assignment design).
  • Clarify the workshop arc and streamline activities so participants can connect critical AI literacy to concrete assignment tweaks.
  • Maintain the well-organized format participants praised.
